Conjunctions / 连词

不但 vs 不仅/不光

not only — three ways to say it, slight differences

Comparison / 对比

不但 búdàn不仅/不光 bùjǐn/bùguāng
RegisterStandard / written不仅: formal; 不光: spoken
Pair with而且 / 还而且 / 还 / 也
Same subject不但 after subject: 他不但…而且…Same rule applies
Diff subjects不但 before subject: 不但他…而且她也…Same rule applies

Examples / 例句

不但: 她不但会说中文，而且会写书法。
Tā búdàn huì shuō zhōngwén, érqiě huì xiě shūfǎ.
She not only speaks Chinese but also writes calligraphy. (standard)
不仅/不光: 他不仅是我的老师，还是我的朋友。
Tā bùjǐn shì wǒ de lǎoshī, hái shì wǒ de péngyou.
He is not only my teacher but also my friend. (slightly more formal)
Quick rule: All three mean 'not only' and are usually interchangeable. Key rule: when two clauses have the SAME subject, put 不但/不仅/不光 AFTER the subject. When subjects DIFFER, put it BEFORE the first subject.

Quick Quiz / 小测验

小王通过了考试，小李也通过了。（两个主语不同）
Different subjects → 不但 before the first subject. (不仅 also works but 不但 is most standard for exams.)
这家餐厅菜好吃，服务也很好。
Written/formal context → 不仅 fits well. (不光 sounds too casual for written text.)
他会开车，___会修车。
Same subject, standard usage → 不但…而且/还… All three work here.
